Biography

I finished my PhD at the Department of Computer Sciences at Brunel University London. I worked in Defence & Cyber Security (DCS) research group, studying the relationship between cyber attacks and real-world events. Specifically, we fused data from multiple sources on the web to pin-point identifying events of various layers of the kill-chain. We applied NLP to social data to identify 'cyber-related discussions' and built an analytical framework for analysing cyber-related data from multiple sources. I am currently researching ML and AI techniques for cyber security at the Center for Cyber Security Research at Cardiff University.

I’m interested in applying ML and AI methods to cyber defence. I have just began investigations into the use of ML in cyber defence and malware prevention. I’m passionate about working with dynamic teams that make little but significant impact in the subject area. This means a steady paced environment with clear cut goals but also flexible to the very dynamic analytical domain (cyber security).

When I’m not in doing research, I contribute to facilitating the inclusion of women in STEM areas as an instructor for Code First Girls UK. I am especially honoured to be given the opportunity to help young women in the UK build static, dynamic websites, web applications and structured databases. I also contribute to multiple remote python development teams across Africa, United States and the UK.

When I do not feel like participating in intellectual work, you will find me baking, airsofting or in a gym somwhere.
